# Admissions Strategy Rubric

Use this rubric to decide whether a plan is Top10/Top20-grade.

## Academic Spike

Strong evidence includes:

- highest course rigor available
- A/A- trend in core courses
- strong AP/IB/A-Level results
- SAT/ACT score matching target tier
- subject competitions or advanced projects

Weak evidence includes:

- course difficulty unclear
- B or lower in major-relevant courses without recovery
- test plan vague
- inflated claims without score reports

## Intellectual Vitality

Strong evidence includes:

- a research question the student can explain
- data and controls
- iteration after failure
- readable technical output
- mentor support but clear student contribution

Weak evidence includes:

- institution-packaged project with no personal contribution
- no data
- no comparison group
- AI buzzwords without measurable effect

## Impact Spike

Strong evidence includes:

- role progression
- system built or improved
- measurable participation/outcome
- third-party evidence
- connection to personal story

Weak evidence includes:

- hours only
- generic volunteering
- passive participation
- no evidence or metrics

## Strategy realism

A good strategy includes:

- main 3 schools with trigger conditions
- fallback ED/EA/RD scenarios
- test-required/test-optional logic
- budget/aid implications if applicable
- safety schools that are actually plausible and affordable
