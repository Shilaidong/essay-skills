# End-to-End Workflow

## 1. Intake

Collect or infer, then mark missing:

- Identity: name, current grade, graduation year, target entry year.
- School context: school name, city, curriculum, school type, grading scale, ranking policy, historical outcomes if available.
- Academics: GPA, rank, transcript, course rigor, exam plan, academic strengths and weak points.
- Testing: SAT/ACT, TOEFL/IELTS/Duolingo, AP/IB/A-Level/national exams, test dates, score reports.
- Activities: leadership, competitions, research, internships, service, athletics, arts, family responsibilities, work, personal projects.
- Application goals: country, degree, major directions, school tier, geography, budget, aid need, ED/EA/REA/RD willingness.
- Deliverables: report type, language, length, Word/PDF/Markdown, charts, cases, citations, font, deadline.

Classify each datum:

- `VERIFIED`: supported by uploaded document or official source.
- `STUDENT_REPORTED`: stated by the student/family/counselor.
- `TARGET`: future goal.
- `ASSUMPTION`: temporary planning assumption.
- `MISSING`: necessary but unavailable.

## 2. Evidence ledger

Before writing recommendations, build a ledger. The ledger prevents the report from turning aspirations into claims.

Minimum columns:

```text
claim, category, status, source_or_proof, date_checked, implication, follow_up, owner, deadline
```

Use the ledger to decide which parts of the report can be written as facts and which must be presented as goals or action items.

## 3. Research

Research only after knowing what the strategy needs. For each school/program, capture:

- Exact program name.
- Application unit: university, college, department, major, faculty, school.
- Curriculum fit and evidence.
- Required/recommended subjects.
- Testing and English policy.
- Portfolio/audition/interview/writing sample/SOP rules.
- Application rounds and restrictions.
- Financial aid/scholarship posture.
- Deadlines and date checked.

## 4. Diagnosis

Write two views:

### Current read

How the applicant appears today.

### Target read

How the applicant should appear by submission.

Then write the bridge: the specific academic, testing, activity, writing, and verification work needed.

## 5. Route architecture

Choose the simplest structure that fits the facts:

- Single-track for a clear applicant.
- Dual-track when the student is legitimately choosing between two directions.
- Multi-track only when the admissions systems require different translation, such as U.K. direct major vs U.S. liberal arts.
- Portfolio route when visual, music, theatre, architecture, or design evidence is required.
- Research/professional route for graduate applications.

Every track needs a decision gate if it will not remain in the final application.

## 6. Development plan

Plan background work with three main lines and one backup:

- Academic/research line.
- Applied product/design/service/professional line.
- Leadership/impact/governance/adoption line.
- Backup line with low permission and low cost.

Each line must have methods, milestones, deliverables, metrics, risks, and evidence.

## 7. School strategy

Create a school/program matrix. Do not rank only by prestige. Tie each school to the applicant's route and evidence.

For each school include:

- Why this school/program.
- What application unit.
- What proof the student needs.
- What risks or requirements matter.
- Which round makes sense.

## 8. Application material strategy

Map evidence to:

- Main essay/personal statement.
- Why major.
- Why school.
- Activity essay.
- Community essay.
- Intellectual vitality essay.
- SOP or research statement.
- Portfolio statement.
- Resume/CV.
- Recommendation packets.

## 9. Timeline

Work backward from deadlines. Include both planning deadlines and internal deadlines.

Use weekly detail for:

- Summer.
- September-November early submission period.
- December-January regular submission period.
- Exam windows.
- Portfolio windows.

## 10. Report production

Write content first, then design. Do not let visual polish hide weak logic.

Recommended sequence:

1. Markdown draft.
2. Tables and ledgers.
3. Gantt image.
4. DOCX generation.
5. Visual QA.
6. Final delivery.
