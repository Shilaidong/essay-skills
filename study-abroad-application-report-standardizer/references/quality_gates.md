# Quality Gates and Rubric

## Non-negotiable gates

The report is not deliverable if any gate fails:

1. Current admissions facts are not verified or clearly marked as needing verification.
2. Student achievements and future targets are mixed together.
3. School/program names are vague or inaccurate.
4. Early application strategy ignores restrictions or budget.
5. Activities lack concrete deliverables and verification paths.
6. Cases are fabricated or disguised as proprietary when sourced publicly.
7. Timeline is impossible under the student's academic load.
8. Word/PDF has visible layout defects.
9. Personal data is exposed unnecessarily.
10. The report implies guaranteed admission.

## 100-point rubric

| Category | Points |
|---|---:|
| Intake completeness and missing-item handling | 10 |
| Source quality and current verification | 15 |
| Admissions-reader diagnosis | 15 |
| Route/major strategy | 10 |
| School/program fit and round strategy | 15 |
| Academic/testing plan | 10 |
| Activity/research/project design | 15 |
| Timeline and execution realism | 5 |
| Document design and readability | 5 |

Minimum client-ready standard: 85.

## Common defects

- “Top school” language without exact program fit.
- Generic activities that any student could do.
- Overclaiming impact.
- Ignoring budget and aid.
- Treating optional portfolio as mandatory, or mandatory portfolio as optional.
- Using old testing policy.
- No decision gate for dual-track applicants.
- No weekly plan near deadlines.
- Wide tables unreadable in Word.

## Final self-check prompts

Ask:

- What would an admissions reader remember after 5 minutes?
- Which claim would be challenged if the family asked for proof?
- Which recommendation depends on a fact that could have changed?
- Which activity is most likely to fail, and what is the backup?
- Does each school have a clear reason beyond prestige?
- Can the student actually execute this schedule?
