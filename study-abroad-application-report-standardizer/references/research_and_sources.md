# Research and Source Policy

## Source hierarchy

Use the strongest available source:

1. Official admissions page.
2. Official department/program page.
3. Official course catalog or bulletin.
4. Official testing organization.
5. Official scholarship/financial aid page.
6. Official government or visa page.
7. University news/profile page.
8. Competition, conference, journal, or award page.
9. Credible first-person interview or project site.
10. Secondary admissions blogs, forums, social media, or rankings.

Use secondary sources only for context or when primary sources are unavailable. Mark lower confidence.

## Facts that require current verification

Always verify when tools permit:

- Application deadlines.
- ED/EA/REA restrictions.
- SAT/ACT/Test-Optional/Test-Required policy.
- English-language requirements and waiver conditions.
- Required courses and subject prerequisites.
- Portfolio/audition/interview/writing sample rules.
- Tuition, financial aid, scholarship, cost of attendance.
- Visa/work rules.
- Recently renamed majors, new programs, suspended programs, or changing admissions units.

If current verification is impossible, write:

```text
需在提交前以学校官网重新核验。
```

## Source ledger fields

Use `assets/source_ledger_template.csv`.

Fields:

```text
id, institution, country, program_or_policy, claim, source_type, url_or_file, date_checked, confidence, notes
```

## Citation style in reports

Choose the style appropriate to the deliverable:

- Client-facing Chinese report: source note table or concise parenthetical source names.
- Academic-style report: footnotes/endnotes.
- Internal working file: URL ledger.

Do not paste raw tool citation tokens into Word.

## Case and benchmark research

For successful applicant cases:

- Use cases to identify patterns, not formulas.
- Preserve provenance in the internal source ledger.
- Anonymize only what is not necessary.
- Do not turn public cases into fictional proprietary cases.
- Do not quote long passages.
- Separate verified details from strategic interpretation.

## Uncertainty language

Use precise uncertainty:

- “根据当前官网信息……”
- “该政策每年可能变化，需在申请季前复核。”
- “学生报告称……，需成绩单/证书确认。”
- “这是目标成果，不是当前成果。”
